Seven American service members have died in the Iran conflict. About 140 more have been wounded.

Behind each of those numbers is a family. A mother who got the phone call no parent should receive. A spouse putting on a brave face for the kids. A community preparing a funeral with a flag-draped casket and a 21-gun salute.

War has a way of cutting through the noise and reminding us what actually matters.

## The Tradition of the American Soldier

America's military tradition is inseparable from its faith tradition. From the Continental Army to the beaches of Normandy to the mountains of Afghanistan, American soldiers have gone into harm's way carrying something more than a rifle. They carry a sense of purpose — a belief that what they're protecting is worth protecting.

That belief is rooted in family, community, and faith. It's not something that can be manufactured by policy or mandated by regulation. It comes from the culture — from homes where service is honored, where sacrifice is recognized, and where the flag means something.

## What We Owe Them

When the nation sends its sons and daughters into combat, it takes on a debt that can never be fully repaid. But we can start by paying attention.

Know their names. Follow the news not as entertainment but as civic duty. Support the organizations serving veterans and military families. Pray for those in harm's way, whatever your tradition.

And when the politics of this conflict get loud — and they will — remember that behind the strategy debates and the cable news arguments are real human beings doing extraordinarily hard things so that the rest of us don't have to.

## A Prayer for the Deployed

This week, wherever you worship, take a moment to pray for the men and women serving in the Middle East. For their safety. For their families. For their leaders to make wise decisions.

They are the best of us. They deserve our attention, our gratitude, and our prayers.
