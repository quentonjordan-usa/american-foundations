If you're trying to keep up with the Iran war, you're not alone. Here's a plain-English breakdown of what happened and where things stand.

## How We Got Here

For years, Iran has been pursuing a nuclear weapons program. The Trump administration entered 2026 determined to stop it — through diplomacy if possible, military force if necessary.

Three rounds of nuclear talks in Oman and Geneva through late February produced some progress but no deal. Iran insisted on its right to enrich uranium. The US demanded a complete end to enrichment. When diplomacy stalled, the military option moved forward.

On February 28, the United States and Israel launched coordinated strikes targeting Iran's nuclear infrastructure, military leadership, and missile program. Iran's Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei was killed in the opening strikes. His son Mojtaba was appointed to replace him.

## Where Things Stand Now

Two weeks in, the strikes are continuing. US Central Command has hit over 6,000 targets inside Iran. Iran has retaliated with waves of drones and missiles against Israel, US military bases in the region, and Gulf state infrastructure.

Seven American service members have been killed. Roughly 140 have been wounded. Iran reports over 1,400 civilian casualties, a figure that is difficult to independently verify.

The Strait of Hormuz has been disrupted, sending oil prices sharply higher. Dubai's airport was struck by an Iranian drone. Qatar temporarily closed its airspace.

## What to Watch

President Trump has said the war could end soon, citing limited remaining targets inside Iran. Iran's new supreme leader has vowed continued retaliation. Congress is demanding hearings. Allies are pushing for diplomacy.

The next two weeks will determine whether this ends as a decisive strike that eliminates Iran's nuclear threat — or expands into a broader regional war.

That distinction matters enormously. Stay informed.
